Christian Berger

Associate Professor & Docent for Software Engineering

University of Gothenburg

Dr. Christian Berger is Associate Professor and Docent for Software Engineering at the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at University of Gothenburg, Sweden. His research focuses on systematically architecting complex software and systems embracing continuous integration (CI), continuous deployment (CD), and continuous experimentation (CE) for a growingly automated and digitalized society.

He is an expert for autonomous systems and self-driving vehicles with over a decade of experience:

  • Coordinating “Team CarOLO” (summary|videos) from Technische Universität Braunschweig, Germany - the best European contestant at the 2007 DARPA Urban Challenge
  • Collaborating with Berkeley’s Drive Lab (summary)
  • Supervising student teams from Chalmers University of Technology and University of Gothenburg at CaroloCup student competition for self-driving miniature cars: 2013 (first prize), 2014, 2015, 2016, 2017
  • Supporting “Chalmers Truck Team” at 2016 Grand Cooperative Driving Challenge (first prize for best HMI)
  • Co-coordinating the research project COPPLAR to develop a self-driving vehicle for urban scenarios
  • Supporting Wallenberg Autonomous Systems and Software Program (WASP) as member of the Graduate School Management Group Autonomous Systems and the Demonstrator Management Group

He is the lead engineer for the world’s first single-file, header-only C++ middleware libcluon (docs|tutorials|source|Docker) and jointly leads the design and development of the open source platform Open Driverless Vehicle (OpenDLV) (source|Twitter). This software powers research projects at Chalmers vehicle laboratory Revere.
